Jimmy Wales’s Vision for Wikipedia

To create a free, open, and collaborative platform for sharing knowledge and information. He believes that by providing free and open access to knowledge, we can create a more informed, empowered, and equitable society. One of Wales’s key goals for Wikipedia is to ensure that it remains an objective and reliable source of information. He has worked to promote a culture of fact-checking, verification, and accountability among contributors, and has been a vocal advocate for using high-quality sources and reliable information. Wales also envisions Wikipedia as a tool for global collaboration and understanding. He believes that by bringing together people from diverse backgrounds and perspectives, we can create a more inclusive and tolerant world. To this end, he has worked to promote multilingualism on Wikipedia and to encourage contributors from around the world to participate in the project.

Wales’ ongiong revolution

In addition to co-founding Wikipedia, Jimmy Wales has been involved in several other internet-related projects and initiatives. Here are some notable examples:

  • Wikia: In 2004, Wales founded Wikia, a for-profit wiki hosting service that allows anyone to create and share content on various topics. The platform has grown to include more than 300,000 communities and has become one of the largest collaborative content creation platforms on the internet. The People’s Operator: In 2012, Wales co-founded The People’s Operator (TPO), a mobile virtual network operator that aimed to donate a portion of its profits to various charitable causes. While the company struggled financially and eventually shut down in 2019, its mission and social impact have inspired other similar ventures in the telecommunications industry.
  • WikiTribune: In 2017, Wales launched WikiTribune, a news website that aimed to provide high-quality, reliable, and transparent journalism by leveraging the collaborative power of the wiki model. While the site is no longer active, it has influenced the conversation around the future of journalism and the role of community-driven initiatives in shaping the media landscape.
  • Campaign for "social network neutrality" - a campaign to ensure that social networking sites do not restrict access to content based on the identity or beliefs of the user. Wales has spoken out against the use of algorithms to filter or prioritize content on social media platforms.
